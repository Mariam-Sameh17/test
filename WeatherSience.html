Temperature Calculations

Temperature predictions are generated using spatial interpolation of satellite data combined with historical patterns for the same time period in previous years.

How Temperature Maps Are Created

Our system processes infrared data from NASA satellites to calculate surface temperatures across the globe.

T_predicted = Σ(historical_T × seasonal_weight) + altitude_adjustment + urban_heat_correction

The algorithm considers multiple factors including elevation, proximity to water bodies, and urban development which can create heat islands.

Rainfall Prediction Models

Rainfall probability is calculated using atmospheric data including humidity, pressure systems, and cloud formation patterns.

Precipitation Probability

Our models analyze multiple factors to determine the likelihood of rainfall:

P(rain) = f(temperature, humidity, pressure, historical_patterns, cloud_cover)

The system compares current conditions with historical data from the same location and time period to generate accurate probability estimates.

  • Cloud formation analysis using satellite imagery
  • Atmospheric pressure gradient calculations
  • Humidity level tracking
  • Historical precipitation patterns

Wind Pattern Analysis

Wind predictions are generated by analyzing pressure systems and their movements across regions.

Understanding Wind Patterns

Wind is caused by differences in atmospheric pressure. Our system tracks these pressure systems to predict wind direction and speed.

Wind Vector = Pressure Gradient × Coriolis Effect + Friction

Key factors in wind prediction:

  • Pressure gradient force
  • Coriolis effect (Earth's rotation)
  • Surface friction
  • Topographical influences

Advanced Atmospheric Equations

Explore the fundamental mathematical equations that govern atmospheric behavior and enable accurate weather predictions.

The Momentum Equation (Navier-Stokes Equation for the atmosphere)

This is essentially Newton's Second Law (F=ma) for air. It describes how forces in the atmosphere create wind (acceleration). The main forces are the pressure gradient, gravity, and the Coriolis effect (from Earth's rotation). It predicts: Wind speed and direction.

∂v/∂t = −(1/ρ)∇p − g*k − 2(Ω × v) + Ffriction

The Continuity Equation (Mass Conservation)

This equation ensures that mass is conserved in the atmosphere. It describes how air density changes with time and how air moves from areas of high pressure to low pressure.

∂ρ/∂t + ∇·(ρv) = 0

The Thermodynamic Energy Equation

This equation describes how temperature changes in the atmosphere due to heating, cooling, and adiabatic processes. It's essential for predicting temperature variations and cloud formation.

∂T/∂t + v·∇T = (1/ρcₚ)(∂p/∂t + v·∇p) + Q/cₚ
